We're building a website that recommends the best places to live based on the user's unique needs and interests. Users will fill out a simple, short profile that will capture their interests, preferences, and priorities and allow the site to match them with locations that best meet their unique lifestyle needs. To support the matching algorithm, the site will use not only standard fact based data about neighborhoods, towns, and cities (climate, crime statistics, cost of living, etc) but will also rely heavily on input and reviews from people living there.

This database of input from current residents will provide a unique profile of what it's really like to live in a given town, and the site's matching algorithm will combine this with the preference data of the relocating user to create the strongest matches. The overall result will be a highly tailored set of recommendations including less obvious places that users may not have considered. Even after the initial list of recommendations is generated, users can easily refine their profiles and adjust the priorities of different attributes to update the recommendations.

FAQ

What's unique about the new site compared to other online "best places" lists?
Most of the existing sites that provide location recommendations are simple lists of desirable locations as chosen by the site creators, or based on some generic poll. Our site will do sophisticated dynamic matching that includes input from current residents on a wide range of important lifestyle attributes.
How will you collect input from current residents?
The site will actively solicit input from current residents much as other product and service review sites do. This data will be aggregated to provide the most accurate view possible of the important aspects of what it's really like to live somewhere.
